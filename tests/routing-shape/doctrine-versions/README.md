**Verdict (2026-07-06):** the essay was actively harmful, not merely ignored: the same
concepts scored 0/3 as accumulated prose and 3/3 as a numbered procedure, with v1 in
between. Models follow procedures and skim essays; every future doctrine change should
compress, never append. v4 adopted.

**Post-assessment patch:** the shipped v4 gained a fourth tie-break (interns-or-workflows
is scale: a handful of units vs dozens-plus frozen, bulk beyond one context, or
verification as the deliverable) after the 100-note job routed 🐜-with-batches; the
compression had folded the workflow-earning criteria into the definition but out of the
procedure, and the regression probe proved procedures are what get followed. The
hackathon tie-breaks were untouched. The backup here is the shipped state.

(A transient v2, v1 plus the interns-logic rebuttal alone, scored 2/3 🤝-announced with
one hybrid marker; its text is reconstructable as v3 minus the spine passage.)

The question the assessment answers: does the same doctrine content route the crew-shaped
build to 🤝 more reliably as a compact procedure than as accumulated persuasion, and does
v1's lighter touch actually outperform both? Interpret with K=3 humility; the boundary is
known to be noisy.
